Why · How it works

It reads your machine, and stops there.

You already pay for these agents. The quota lives in plain files on your own disk — you just can't see it while you work.

Kaji Gauge fixes only that. A small, dependency-free Python reader parses the local Claude and Codex usage files and hands the numbers to the gauge. That's the whole machine.

Nothing leaves your machine. No account, no telemetry, no network call. Local files in, rings out.
  • Read

    A bundled Python reader — no pip, no dependencies — opens the local Claude and Codex usage files.

  • Parse

    It works out what's used and what's left for each provider, on the spot.

  • Draw

    The native app renders a warm ring per agent in the menu bar and the floating panel.

  • Refresh

    It re-reads on its own, quietly, so the rings stay honest while you keep coding.
